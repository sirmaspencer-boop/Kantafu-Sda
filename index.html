Our Identity

Who We Are & Our Mission

Who We Are

Mountain View Seventh-day Adventist Church in Kantafu is a warm, Spirit-led congregation rooted in the Word of God. We are part of the global Seventh-day Adventist Church — a worldwide family of over 22 million believers in more than 215 countries.

We believe in whole-person ministry — nurturing the body, mind, and spirit. Our doors are open to everyone, regardless of background, because we believe every person bears the image of God.

"A place where everyone belongs — built on the mountain, called to the community."

Our Mission

Making Disciples of All Nations

To proclaim the everlasting Gospel of Jesus Christ, prepare a people for His soon return, and serve our community with compassion, integrity, and hope — body, mind and soul.

Our Vision

A Transformed Kantafu

A community transformed by the love of God — where every family knows Christ, every person is healthy and whole, and the name of Jesus is lifted high across Kantafu and beyond.

Roots & Heritage

How the SDA Church Began

1844
The Great Disappointment — On October 22, 1844, thousands of Millerite Adventists expected Christ's return. When He did not come as expected, most gave up. A small group searched the Scriptures more carefully and discovered a new understanding of the heavenly sanctuary (Daniel 8:14).
1844–50
Bible Study & Revelation — A small band of believers including James White, Ellen G. White, and Joseph Bates studied the Bible intensely. They recovered the seventh-day Sabbath truth, the state of the dead, and other foundational doctrines.
1863
Formal Organization — The General Conference of Seventh-day Adventists was officially organized on May 21, 1863, in Battle Creek, Michigan, with approximately 3,500 members and 125 churches. God's remnant church had taken shape.
1870s–1900s
Global Expansion — Missionaries were sent around the world. Schools, hospitals, and publishing houses were established on every continent. The church grew rapidly in Africa, Asia, Latin America and the Pacific.
Early 1900s
SDA in Kenya — Adventist missionaries arrived in East Africa, planting churches and schools across Kenya. The church became one of the largest Christian denominations in the country, with strong presence in education and health.
Today
Mountain View SDA, Kantafu — Our local congregation grew out of the faithfulness of early Adventist pioneers in this region. Today we carry that same mission — preaching the everlasting Gospel and preparing a people for Christ's return.

Doctrine & Truth

What We Believe — 28 Fundamental Beliefs

The Seventh-day Adventist Church holds 28 Fundamental Beliefs, each grounded in Scripture. Here are the key pillars of our faith:

01
The Holy Scriptures

The Bible is the inspired Word of God — our supreme authority in all matters of faith and practice. (2 Tim. 3:16)

02
The Trinity

One God: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — a unity of three co-eternal Persons. (Matt. 28:19)

03
God the Father

An eternal, personal God of love, justice, mercy and grace — Creator and Sustainer of all. (Gen. 1:1)

04
God the Son

Jesus Christ is truly God and truly human — our Saviour, Lord, and coming King. (John 1:1, 14)

05
God the Holy Spirit

The Spirit convicts, transforms, and empowers believers and the church. (Acts 1:8)

06
Creation

God created the world in six literal days and rested on the seventh. Life is a gift from the Creator. (Gen. 1–2)

07
The Nature of Humanity

Humans were created in God's image — body, mind, and spirit as an inseparable whole. (Gen. 1:26–27)

08
The Great Controversy

A cosmic conflict between Christ and Satan underlies all of human history. We live in its final chapter. (Rev. 12)

09
The Life, Death & Resurrection of Christ

Christ's perfect life, atoning death, and bodily resurrection are the foundation of our salvation. (1 Cor. 15:3–4)

10
The Sabbath

The seventh day (Saturday) Sabbath is God's eternal sign of rest, creation, and redemption. (Ex. 20:8–11)

11
Baptism

Baptism by immersion is a public declaration of death to sin and new life in Christ. (Rom. 6:1–6)

12
The Second Coming

Christ will return literally, visibly, and gloriously. This is our blessed hope. (Acts 1:11; Rev. 1:7)

Biblical Truth

What Is the Soul According to the Bible?

Many people believe the soul is immortal and lives on after death. But what does the Bible actually say? This is one of the most important — and most misunderstood — doctrines in Christianity.

📖 What the Bible Says

  • • Genesis 2:7 — "God formed man from the dust and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life, and man became a living soul." (A soul is not something you have — it is what you ARE.)
  • • Ezekiel 18:4 — "The soul that sins, it shall die." Souls are mortal.
  • • Ecclesiastes 9:5 — "The dead know nothing." Death is like sleep, not conscious existence.
  • • John 11:11–14 — Jesus described the dead Lazarus as "sleeping," then raised him at the resurrection.
  • • 1 Thessalonians 4:16 — The dead are raised at Christ's Second Coming — not immediately at death.

✅ What SDAs Believe

Seventh-day Adventists believe in conditional immortality — also called "soul sleep." Human beings are mortal. At death, the person is unconscious, waiting for the resurrection. Immortality is not something we naturally possess — it is a gift given at the Second Coming of Christ to those who have accepted Him as Saviour.

"The living know that they will die, but the dead know nothing." — Ecclesiastes 9:5

This truth protects against false teachings like communicating with the dead (spiritualism) and purgatory.

God's Law

The Ten Commandments

Found in Exodus 20:1–17, the Ten Commandments are God's eternal moral law — a transcript of His character, and a guide for holy living. SDAs uphold all ten, including the fourth — the seventh-day Sabbath.

I
No Other Gods

"You shall have no other gods before Me." — We worship only the Creator God.

II
No Idols

"You shall not make for yourself an idol." — We worship God in spirit and truth, not through images.

III
God's Name

"You shall not misuse the name of the LORD your God." — We honour God's name in word and deed.

IV
Remember the Sabbath

"Remember the Sabbath day by keeping it holy. Six days you shall labour… but the seventh day is a Sabbath to the LORD." — Saturday is God's holy rest day.

V
Honour Parents

"Honour your father and your mother." — Strong families are the foundation of strong communities.

VI
Do Not Murder

"You shall not murder." — Human life is sacred, made in God's image.

VII
Be Faithful

"You shall not commit adultery." — Marriage is a sacred covenant between one man and one woman.

VIII
Do Not Steal

"You shall not steal." — Respect others' property and work with honesty and integrity.

IX
Do Not Lie

"You shall not give false testimony." — Speak truth in all things; be trustworthy.

X
Do Not Covet

"You shall not covet." — Be content with what God has given you; avoid jealousy and greed.

"If you love Me, keep My commandments." — Jesus Christ (John 14:15)

SDA Lifestyle

Do's & Don'ts of the SDA Faith

SDA lifestyle guidelines are not about earning salvation — they flow from gratitude to God and a desire to honour Him with our whole being. "Whether you eat or drink, do it all to the glory of God." — 1 Cor. 10:31

✅ Do's

  • 🙏 Worship God on the seventh-day Sabbath (Saturday)
  • 📖 Study the Bible daily and pray without ceasing
  • 🥦 Follow a healthy plant-based diet (Daniel 1)
  • 💧 Drink plenty of water; follow the 8 health laws
  • 🤝 Serve your community and care for the needy
  • 💰 Return faithful tithe and offerings to God
  • 👗 Dress modestly and with dignity
  • 🏥 Practise preventive health — exercise, rest, sunshine
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 Honour marriage as a sacred covenant
  • 🌱 Care for God's creation and the environment

❌ Don'ts

  • 🚬 Avoid tobacco in any form (1 Cor. 6:19–20)
  • 🍺 Abstain from alcohol and harmful substances
  • 🥩 Avoid unclean meats as defined in Leviticus 11
  • 🎰 Avoid gambling and games of chance
  • 📺 Avoid entertainment that glorifies sin and violence
  • 💔 Avoid sexual immorality — keep intimacy within marriage
  • 🤥 Avoid dishonesty, gossip, and false witness
  • 🛒 Avoid secular commerce on the Sabbath
  • 💊 Avoid addictive drugs and substances
  • ⚔️ Avoid participation in warfare that takes human life

The Spirit of Prophecy

Ellen G. White & Her Teachings

✍️

Ellen G. White

1827 – 1915

Co-founder of the Seventh-day Adventist Church and one of the most translated female authors in history. Her writings are regarded as inspired counsel for the church.

Ellen White received prophetic visions from a young age. Adventists believe her writings are a gift of the Holy Spirit — not equal to Scripture, but a "lesser light" pointing to the "greater light" of the Bible.

📚 Health Reform

Ellen White wrote extensively on diet, lifestyle, and the connection between physical and spiritual health — decades before modern science confirmed many of her insights. Her book The Ministry of Healing remains a SDA classic.

📖 Education

She championed wholistic Christian education — training the hands, heart, and head. Her book Education shaped hundreds of Adventist schools worldwide.

🌍 The Great Controversy

Her masterwork The Great Controversy traces the conflict between Christ and Satan from ancient Israel to Christ's Second Coming — and is widely considered one of the most powerful Christian books ever written.

✝️ Steps to Christ

Her devotional classic Steps to Christ has been translated into more than 165 languages and has led millions to a personal relationship with Jesus.

Freedom of Conscience

Religious Liberty

Seventh-day Adventists are passionate advocates for freedom of religion and conscience. This is not just a political position — it is rooted in the Bible and in the example of Jesus, who never compelled faith.

⚖️

Freedom to Worship

Every human being has the God-given right to worship according to the dictates of their own conscience. No government or institution should compel religious observance.

🕊️

Church & State

SDAs uphold the separation of church and state. History shows that when governments enforce religious laws, persecution follows. We defend this separation as a protection for all.

📰

Liberty Magazine

The SDA Church publishes Liberty Magazine — one of the oldest religious liberty journals in the world — advocating for freedom of conscience for people of all faiths.

"Render to Caesar what is Caesar's, and to God what is God's." — Jesus (Mark 12:17)

Adventists in the World

Famous Seventh-day Adventists

The SDA Church has produced world-class leaders in medicine, public health, education, music, politics, and more. Their faith shaped their contribution to the world.

🏥
Dr. Ben Carson

World-renowned neurosurgeon and former US Secretary of Housing. Known for separating conjoined twins. A devout Adventist whose faith shaped his calling to medicine.

🎵
Little Richard

Rock and roll pioneer who later returned to his Adventist faith and gospel music roots, reflecting on the spiritual foundation of his life.

⚕️
Dr. John Harvey Kellogg

Medical doctor and inventor of corn flakes — a product born from his Adventist health principles at the Battle Creek Sanitarium.

✈️
Desmond Doss

WWII conscientious objector and Medal of Honor recipient who saved 75 soldiers without carrying a weapon. His story was told in the film Hacksaw Ridge.

🎙️
Wintley Phipps

Grammy-nominated gospel artist, pastor, and founder of the US Dream Academy — known for his powerful gospel performances before world leaders.

🔬
Dr. T. Colin Campbell

Nutritional biochemist and author of The China Study — whose landmark research on plant-based diets reflects Adventist health principles.

Spiritual Health & Mental Wellness

🧠 Youth & Men's Mental Awareness

Mental health is not a weakness — it is a health issue. Mountain View SDA Church is committed to breaking the stigma of mental illness and supporting our members, especially our youth and men, with compassion and biblical truth.

  • 🙏 Prayer and spiritual support — connecting struggling members with prayer partners and pastoral care
  • 👥 Support groups — safe spaces for men and youth to share struggles without judgement
  • 📚 Education — mental health awareness programmes, seminars, and resources
  • 🏥 Professional referrals — partnering with Christian counsellors and health professionals

If you are struggling, please speak to our pastor or a church elder. You are not alone. God sees you. We see you.
